Our objective then?

To sum up, preferences delivered value to each stakeholder

But also revealed friction points that required refinement.
Guests
  • Guests could control the type of rooms they were going to stay in.
  • But 30% of requests received no replies, forcing guests to cancel.
Booking.com

The company experienced increased revenue following the expansion of available options.

Partners
  • Partners got a +1.3 uplift reviews score and a decrease in cancellations when requests were honoured.
  • But, as most requests were text-based, processing them was time-consuming, causing 30% to remain unanswered.
Our first step

Bringing customisation to Room selection

When we started, it was not possible to customise a room before reservation checkout.

If we wanted to build a scalable solution, we needed to separate the user request from the feature: customisations needed to live in Base.

When designing for A/B testing, it's crucial to ask: what is the smallest step I can take to validate my hypothesis?
